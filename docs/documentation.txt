Phase 1 Final Report
FROM a project management point of view.
Sports Courts Rental Management System — Business Direction
1. Final Product Vision

The product will start as a free, mobile-first court management system for football courts in Assiut, designed to replace paper-based booking and help court owners manage reservations, deposits, remaining payments, staff activity, and daily/weekly operations.

The long-term vision is to use this management system to build accurate court availability data, then grow into a sports court booking platform where players can discover available slots and book through the platform.

Final vision statement:

A free Arabic-first court management system for football courts in Assiut that helps owners replace paper booking, control staff activity, track payments, and later receive verified player bookings through a platform that takes commission only from bookings it generates.

2. Strategic Positioning

The product should not be positioned as “just another booking app.”

The correct positioning is:

We are starting from the operational side of courts, not from the player marketplace side.

This is important because most local court owners currently use:

Paper notebooks
Phone calls
Walk-ins
Manual deposit tracking
Staff memory
Informal workflows

So the first value is not “players can book online.”

The first value is:

Owner can monitor remotely.
Staff cannot easily manipulate records.
Bookings become organized.
Deposits and remaining payments are visible.
Court schedule becomes digital.
Future player booking becomes possible because availability data becomes reliable.

7. Commission Model
Agreed Commission
Commission: 10% of full booking price
Typical court price in Assiut: 200–300 EGP
Expected platform commission: 20–30 EGP per booking
Example

If booking price is 300 EGP:

Player pays deposit: 50 EGP
Platform commission: 30 EGP
Court receives from deposit: 20 EGP
Player pays remaining amount at court: 250 EGP

Final result:

Player paid total: 300 EGP
Platform earned: 30 EGP
Court received total: 270 EGP

This must be clearly written in the court agreement.

10. Booking Status Rules

The originally suggested statuses were:

Hold
Deposited
Completed
No-show

But this was improved because “Deposited” is more of a payment state than a booking state.

Recommended Booking Statuses
Status	Meaning
Hold	Slot is temporarily held, no deposit confirmed yet
Confirmed	Deposit confirmed and booking is active
Completed	Customer played
Cancelled	Booking was cancelled
No-show	Customer did not come
Expired	Hold expired before deposit
Recommended Payment Statuses
Status	Meaning
Awaiting Deposit	No deposit received yet
Deposit Received	Deposit confirmed
Settlement Pending	Platform owes court money
Settled	Court has been paid
Refunded	Used in court/platform problem cases

This separation is important for clean reports later.

11. Deposit Rules
Court-Side Manual Bookings

For MVP 1:

Staff can enter deposit manually.
Staff can approve booking with or without deposit.
This is the court’s responsibility.
Platform does not interfere in manual court bookings.
Platform/CS Bookings

For MVP 2:

Deposit is required.
Deposit amount differs per court.
Common deposit is around 50 EGP.
Deposit is paid to the project/platform wallet.
CS confirms transaction manually.
Booking is confirmed only after deposit is verified.
12. Hold Rules

For platform bookings:

Customer can hold a slot for 15 minutes.
If deposit is not sent within 15 minutes, the hold expires.
Customer cannot hold more than 3 slots per day before deposit.
Tracking should be by customer phone number.

Recommended rule wording:

A customer can create a temporary hold for 15 minutes. If deposit is not verified before expiry, the hold expires automatically and the slot becomes available again.

13. Settlement Rules

Settlement will happen:

Next morning.

Meaning:

Platform-generated bookings are collected during the day.
Settlement is calculated.
Platform deducts 10% commission.
Remaining amount is transferred to court owner the next morning.
Transaction ID is recorded.

This is better than instant settlement because it reduces:

Small repeated transfers
Human mistakes
Duplicate transfers
Confusing reconciliation
Operational overload
14. Cancellation and No-Show Policy
If Customer Cancels or No-Shows

Agreed rule:

Deposit is lost.

For platform booking:

Platform keeps its 10% commission.
Remaining deposit amount goes to court.

Example:

Booking price: 300 EGP
Deposit: 50 EGP
Platform commission: 30 EGP
Court receives: 20 EGP

The deposit is fully consumed.

If Court Causes the Problem

Examples:

Court is closed.
Court refuses confirmed booking.
Slot was marked available but court cannot honor it.
Court causes failed booking.

Agreed rule:

Customer gets refund or alternative slot.
Court may receive penalty.
Repeated violations can lead to termination from platform.
If Platform/CS Causes the Problem

Examples:

CS confirms wrong slot.
CS records payment incorrectly.
CS confirms booking by mistake.

Recommended rule:

Customer gets refund or alternative slot.
CS error is handled internally.
Internal penalty alone is not enough; the customer must be protected.
15. Source of Truth Rule

This is one of the most important business rules.

Agreed rule:

If the court marks a slot as available in the system, the platform has the right to confirm a booking for that slot.

This must be included in the court agreement.

Meaning:

The court does not need to approve every platform booking manually.
The system schedule is the source of truth.
If the court does not want a slot to be booked, it must block or reserve it in the system.
The platform can sell available slots based on system data.

This is critical for scalability.

16. Public Phone Number Decision

Agreed:

The public player app/platform should not show the court phone number directly.

Reason:

If phone numbers are shown directly:

Players may bypass the platform.
Platform cannot prove booking source.
Commission model becomes weak.
Court owner may avoid paying commission.

Instead, future player flow should use:

Request booking
Book through platform
CS-assisted booking
Future direct app booking
Tracked communication if needed

24. Main Risks
1. Staff Adoption Risk

If the system is slower than paper, staff will not use it.

Mitigation:

Mobile-first
Arabic-first
Fast booking form
Minimal required fields
Clear schedule view
Large buttons
Simple UX
2. Owner Trust Risk

Owners may not trust platform money handling.

Mitigation:

Written agreement
Daily/next-morning settlement
Clear transaction records
Settlement reports
Transparent commission
3. Data Accuracy Risk

If staff does not update availability, platform bookings may fail.

Mitigation:

System is source of truth
Audit logs
Court penalties
Training
Simple blocking/reserving flow
4. Commission Dispute Risk

Court may misunderstand how 10% is deducted.

Mitigation:

Clear signed agreement
Example calculations
Settlement report
Separate platform vs manual bookings
5. Customer Dispute Risk

If court fails booking, customer may blame platform.

Mitigation:

Refund/alternative slot policy
Court penalties
CS support workflow
Clear user communication
6. Operational Load Risk

Manual CS booking does not scale.

Mitigation:

Use CS only as MVP 2 validation
Later move to direct app booking
Later integrate payment gateway
7. Free Product Risk

Free management system may not generate revenue by itself.

Mitigation:

Use it as acquisition channel
Monetize only platform-generated bookings
Keep option for premium features later

26. Final Recommendations
Recommendation 1

Build MVP 1 only as court management.

Do not mix management, marketplace, CS, and payment in the first release.

Recommendation 2

Make the UI mobile-first and Arabic-first.

Your users are local court staff using phones. A desktop-style dashboard will fail.

Recommendation 3

Store audit logs from day one.

This supports your main promise: control and anti-manipulation.

Recommendation 4

Treat the schedule as the source of truth.

This is required for future platform booking.

Recommendation 5

Use signed court agreements before handling platform bookings.

Do not collect money or confirm platform bookings without clear terms.

Recommendation 6

Start with 3–5 courts only.

Watch them for at least 2 weeks.

The real validation question is:

Can a real court use this system for 2 full weeks without returning to paper?

Recommendation 7

Do not show court phone numbers in the future platform.

This protects your booking attribution and commission model.

Recommendation 8

Use CS booking only as a bridge.

Manual CS booking is useful for validation, but the long-term goal should be direct app booking and payment automation.
